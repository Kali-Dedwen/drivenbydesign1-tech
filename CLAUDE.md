## The Seven Must-Haves — Non-Negotiable Execution Standard

Every M2M output — client deliverable, proposal, email, automation, keynote, social post — must carry all seven before it distributes. This is not aspirational. It is structural.

### Quality Layer (five)

| # | Must-Have | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | Trust | Earns the room before asking anything of it |
| 02 | Context | Calibrated to the specific reader, room, and channel |
| 03 | Liability | Nothing ships that exposes M2M, the client, or a third party |
| 04 | Taste | Navy/Gold sovereign aesthetic — always in the cloud of exceptional |
| 05 | Intent | Sovereign objective declared — why before what |

### Doctrine Layer (two)

| # | Must-Have | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| 06 | Intentional Intent | The Architect's 20% is felt in the work — not inferred from it |
| 07 | Fractional Legitimacy | Execution sourced (80%), close owned (20%), output signed |

### Trust vs. Liability Gate

Fires between Must-Have 03 and 04. Binary question: does this output build trust or create liability? If liability — rewrite before distribution. See `skills/m2m-trust-liability-gate/SKILL.md` for the full gate protocol.

### Sign-Off Marker

All authenticated output carries this sign-off:

> Fractional Legitimacy / Intentional Intent — authenticated. To the work.
