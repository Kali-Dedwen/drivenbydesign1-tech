# M2M Quality Standards — The Seven Must-Haves

## Non-Negotiable Execution Standard

Every M2M output — client deliverable, proposal, email, automation, keynote, social post —
must carry all seven before it distributes. This is not aspirational. It is structural.

## Quality Layer

### Must-Have 01 — Trust
Earns the room before asking anything of it.

The output must establish credibility, safety, and resonance before it makes a request,
presents a price, or proposes an action. Trust is not earned by claiming expertise.
It is earned by demonstrating understanding of the reader's situation before asking them
to move. If the first sentence asks for something, the output fails Must-Have 01.

Validation questions:
- Does this open with the reader's world, not M2M's?
- Would the recipient feel understood before they feel sold to?
- Does the credibility signal come from demonstrated competence, not self-declaration?

### Must-Have 02 — Context
Calibrated to the specific reader, room, and channel.

A grant narrative reads differently than a LinkedIn post. An email to a corrections
superintendent reads differently than an email to a venture partner. The same M2M value
proposition must bend to the audience without breaking. Context means the output would
feel wrong if sent to a different audience — because it was built for this one.

Validation questions:
- Would this feel out of place if sent to a different audience or through a different channel?
- Does the language register match the recipient's professional context?
- Are the references, examples, and framing relevant to this specific reader?

### Must-Have 03 — Liability
Nothing ships that exposes M2M, the client, or a third party.

Liability is not just legal. It includes reputational risk, compliance exposure, data
handling violations, and promises the organization cannot keep. If the output contains
a commitment, that commitment must be deliverable. If the output references a partner,
that reference must be authorized. If the output touches regulated territory, governance
skill must be consulted first.

Validation questions:
- Does this make any promise M2M cannot fulfill?
- Does this reference any partner, client, or third party without authorization?
- Does this touch HIPAA, FERPA, federal grant, or compliance-adjacent territory?
- Could this create legal, financial, or reputational exposure for anyone?

---

### Trust vs. Liability Gate

**This gate fires between Must-Have 03 and Must-Have 04. It is binary.**

Does this output build trust or create liability?

- If trust — proceed to Must-Have 04.
- If liability — rewrite before distribution. Do not pass this gate.

See `m2m-trust-liability-gate/SKILL.md` for the full gate protocol.

### Must-Have 04 — Taste
Navy/Gold sovereign aesthetic — always in the cloud of exceptional.

Every M2M output must look and feel like it belongs in the same room as the best work
in its category. Taste is not decoration. It is the signal that tells the reader this
organization operates at a level they can trust with their career, their workforce,
or their funding. Sloppy formatting, generic templates, and misaligned brand elements
destroy trust faster than bad strategy.

Validation questions:
- Does this follow the M2M brand design system — Navy, Gold, typography hierarchy?
- Would this sit comfortably next to the best work in its category?
- Is every visual and textual element intentional, or are there defaults left in place?
- Does this carry the sovereign aesthetic — grounded, earned, deliberate?

### Must-Have 05 — Intent
Sovereign objective declared — why before what.

Every output must declare its purpose before delivering its content. The reader should
know within the first two sentences why this exists and what it is oriented toward.
Intent is not a subject line. It is the strategic reason this output was created,
expressed in a way that respects the reader's time and intelligence.

Validation questions:
- Can the reader identify the purpose of this output within the first two sentences?
- Is the why stated before the what?
- Does the intent align with M2M's strategic objectives, not just the immediate task?

## Doctrine Layer

### Must-Have 06 — Intentional Intent
The Architect's 20% is felt in the work — not inferred from it.

Intentional Intent means the sovereign reasoning of the Architect is encoded into the
output itself. The reader should feel the presence of human strategic judgment —
not just competent automation. This is the difference between a deliverable that was
executed and a deliverable that was authored. The 20% is not a final review pass.
It is embedded in the architecture of the output from the beginning.

Validation questions:
- Does this output carry strategic reasoning that only the Architect could provide?
- Would removing the Architect's contribution reduce this to generic automation output?
- Is the human intelligence visible in the choices made, not just the polish applied?
- Does this reflect the II doctrine — core reasoning, edge case definition, deterministic guardrails?

### Must-Have 07 — Fractional Legitimacy
Execution sourced (80%), close owned (20%), output signed.

Fractional Legitimacy is the operating doctrine that separates M2M from both full-service
consultancies and pure automation shops. The pipeline handles 80% of execution — research,
drafting, formatting, routing. The Sovereign Architect owns the 20% that authenticates —
the strategic judgment, relational credibility, and final signature that makes the output
M2M's and not just anyone's.

Validation questions:
- Was the 80% execution handled by the pipeline with appropriate quality?
- Did the Architect supply the 20% — strategic judgment, relational context, final authentication?
- Is the output signed and attributed, not anonymous?
- Does the FL zone assignment match the sensitivity of this output?

## Seven Must-Haves Checklist

Before any output distributes, confirm all seven:

1. Trust — earns the room before asking anything of it
2. Context — calibrated to the specific reader, room, and channel
3. Liability — nothing ships that exposes M2M, the client, or a third party
4. Gate — trust vs. liability binary check passed
5. Taste — Navy/Gold sovereign aesthetic, cloud of exceptional
6. Intent — sovereign objective declared, why before what
7. Intentional Intent — Architect's 20% is felt in the work
8. Fractional Legitimacy — execution sourced, close owned, output signed

## Sign-Off Marker

All authenticated output carries this sign-off:

> Fractional Legitimacy / Intentional Intent — authenticated. To the work.

This marker is not optional. It is the structural confirmation that all seven must-haves
have been verified and the output has passed through the full quality gate.
